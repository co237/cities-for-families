Change in Under-5 Population by County (April 2020 - July 2024)

About This Data

This map visualizes changes in the under-5 population (children ages 0-4) in major U.S. counties between April 2020 and July 2024. The data reveals a significant decline in young children across most large urban areas.

Definition of "Major Cities"

For this analysis, we define major cities as counties with a total population of 250,000 or more as of April 2020. This threshold captures the primary counties of major metropolitan areas (279 counties total) while excluding smaller rural areas where demographic patterns differ significantly.

Key Findings

  • Large coastal cities hit hardest: New York City boroughs, Los Angeles, San Francisco, and other expensive coastal metros saw the steepest declines
  • NYC & LA dominate losses: New York County (Manhattan) lost 18.9% of its under-5 population; Los Angeles County lost over 80,000 young children
  • Sun Belt gaining: Texas and Florida counties, especially in suburban/exurban areas, saw significant gains
  • Cost of living connection: Many of the counties with the largest percentage declines are also among the most expensive places to live

Data Source

U.S. Census Bureau, Population Division. County Population Estimates by Age and Sex: April 1, 2020 to July 1, 2024 (CC-EST2024-AGESEX). Released June 2025.
