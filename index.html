About This Data

This map visualizes changes in the under-5 population (children ages 0-4) in major U.S. counties between April 2020 and July 2024.

Why focus on counties with 500,000+ population? These 142 counties represent the core of America's largest metropolitan areas. While they contain about 54% of the nation's young children, they account for 69% of the decline—indicating that large urban centers are disproportionately losing families with young children.

Key Findings

  • Large coastal cities hit hardest: New York City boroughs, Los Angeles, San Francisco, and other expensive coastal metros saw the steepest declines
  • NYC & LA dominate losses: New York County (Manhattan) lost 18.9% of its under-5 population; Los Angeles County lost over 80,000 young children
  • Sun Belt gaining: Texas and Florida counties, especially in suburban/exurban areas, saw significant gains
  • Cost of living connection: Many of the counties with the largest percentage declines are also among the most expensive places to live
